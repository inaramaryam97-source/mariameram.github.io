Policy Briefs

Enhancing Aviation's Role in Malaysia's Economic Recovery

Summary (1 page): Post-COVID, Malaysia's aviation sector needs policy reforms to boost GDP. Recommendations include investing RM10 billion in KLIA infrastructure for 20% passenger growth by 2030, reducing taxes on airlines, and promoting green aviation. This could add 1% to GDP annually.

Full Brief (2 pages): The aviation industry is a cornerstone of Malaysia's economy, contributing RM50 billion in 2022. Challenges include competition from regional hubs and environmental regulations. Policies should focus on digitalization, partnerships with ASEAN, and subsidies for domestic carriers. Expected outcomes: Job creation and increased tourism revenue.

Comparing KLIA and Regional Hubs: Policy Implications

Summary (1 page): KLIA trails Changi in efficiency. Policies: Adopt Singapore's model of privatization and tech integration to cut costs by 15%, potentially increasing GDP contribution to RM30 billion.

Full Brief (2 pages): Data shows KLIA handles fewer flights than peers. Policy brief recommends benchmarking against Changi, investing in AI for operations, and opening to more low-cost carriers. This aligns with Malaysia's 12th Plan for sustainable growth.
